Why Do We Need Governance?

The Governance process ensures structured communication between all project participants. It's a system of meetings, roles, and decision-making processes that helps:

Transparency

  • Visible progress tracking
  • Clear goals and metrics
  • Accessible information

Decision Efficiency

  • Fast decision making
  • Clear escalation paths
  • Right people at right time

Strategic Alignment

  • Connect strategy to execution
  • Resource prioritization
  • Business goals achievement

How We Work: Sprint Planning Without CTO

Reluna Approach to Technical Leadership

We don't have a CTO. Instead, we distribute technical leadership across the team with clear roles and responsibilities:

Sprint Planning Process

1. Product Owner
Brings prioritized backlog, defines "what" and "why"
2. Tech Lead
Provides technical feasibility, architecture guidance, estimates "how"
3. Scrum Master
Facilitates the process, removes blockers, ensures team commitment
4. Dev Team
Estimates effort, identifies risks, commits to sprint goal
Tech Lead provides technical direction, team owns decisions collectively through Tech Sync and AI Guild, Scrum ceremonies ensure structure, no single-person bottleneck.

Sprint Flow: 2 Weeks Cycle

Input
  • • Prioritized Product Backlog
  • • User Stories with Acceptance Criteria
  • • Technical Debt Items
  • • Bug Fixes
  • • Team Velocity (from previous sprints)
→
Sprint (2 weeks)
  • • Day 1: Sprint Planning (2h)
  • • Daily: Standups (15min)
  • • Mid-sprint: Tech Sync, Portfolio & Design Sync
  • • Last Day: Sprint Review (1h)
  • • Last Day: Retrospective (1.5h)
→
Output
  • • Working Product Increment
  • • Deployed Features (to staging/prod)
  • • Updated Documentation
  • • Sprint Report & Metrics
  • • Updated Backlog (refined & reprioritized)
  • • Team Retrospective Action Items
Sprint Goal: Each sprint produces a potentially shippable product increment. Team commits to specific user stories during Sprint Planning, delivers working features by sprint end, and demonstrates progress in Sprint Review.

📋 Release Strategy

Parallel Development: Both portals are developed simultaneously by the same team, with bi-weekly sprints alternating focus between products.

User Feedback Loop: Each MVP is released to a small group of early adopters for testing and feedback before the next sprint cycle.

Continuous Deployment: Features are deployed to staging after each sprint review and to production after stakeholder approval.

Meeting Structure

Five Types of Meetings in Our Governance

Our meeting structure is organized into five levels, each serving a specific purpose in our governance model:

Strategic Level

Long-term vision, investments, business direction, shareholder decisions.

Meetings: Board Meeting, QBR, Business Performance Review
Participants: Shareholder, Leadership, Strategy

Product Level

Product strategy, roadmap alignment, feature priorities, portfolio management.

Meetings: Product Sync (Both Portals), Portfolio & Design Sync
Participants: Product Owners, CPO, Dev Team

Go-to-Market Level

Marketing, customer insights, growth strategy, user acquisition & retention.

Meetings: Marketing & Growth, Customer Insights, Growth Strategy Session
Participants: Marketing, Product Owners, Strategy

Technical Level

Architecture decisions, technical debt, AI innovation, design systems, code quality.

Meetings: Tech Sync, AI Engineering Guild
Participants: Tech Lead, Engineers, Designer, AI Champion

Operational Level (Scrum)

Daily coordination, sprint planning & execution, team retrospectives, demos.

Meetings: Daily Standups, Sprint Planning, Sprint Review, Retrospective
Participants: Scrum Master, Product Owner, Dev Team
